Summary

The Glass House is everything Neuroplex dreamed. The walls are transparent and there's nowhere for anyone to hide. In the Neuralverse, that means it's ready for primetime.

Five women arrive at an undisclosed location.

Kimberly is a real estate agent in her forties, boastful about her ability to sell anything, including herself. Madison is an actress, frustrated by years of minor roles and lack of commercial success. She's willing to compete for the recognition she craves. The barista Hannah is deferential but cunning beneath her quiet demeanor. Ashley the gaming streamer thinks she's got this; she's confident about her status and understanding of audience dynamics. Zara is a programmer, the most mysterious of the group, making coded references to her AI research. She's the puzzle box of the group.

April, an AI voice synthesizer, explains the game with corporate efficiency and what sounds like glee. The rules are simple: compete, win, or be voted out. But the real rule is never stated: adaptation is survival. Kitty, the previous season's champion, moves through the Glass House as someone who's already submitted and found liberation in that surrender.

The contestants begin to reorganize themselves around the systems that expose them. They stop asking whether they should be visible and start asking how to perform visibility effectively. They form alliances based on who understands the game's logic best.

The Glass House is a transformation chamber. The transparent walls aren't for the audience. They're for the architects to see exactly how thoroughly each contestant can be remade.
