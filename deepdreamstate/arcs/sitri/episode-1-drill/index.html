Summary

At the Sitri Center, scientists have moved beyond passive observation to active dream engineering.

Dr. Tessa Finn, newly arrived from the corporate world, begins her first clinical trial with subject Phoebe Bosworth. Phoebe’s anxiety dreams have become saturated with shame and arousal. Tessa deploys the prototype Construct Thirty-Seven.

Dr. Meg Aerin, a skeptical and territorial senior researcher, challenges Tessa's methodology. She says Phoebe is learning addiction instead of resilience. The tension escalates when the two scientists make a high-stakes wager: one week to prove whether Tessa's intervention will help or harm Phoebe, with a twelve-million-dollar research budget on the line.

As Phoebe's dreams intensify, manifesting as vivid scenarios where public exposure and erotic humiliation intertwine, the boundaries between therapeutic intervention and manipulation blur.

Tessa's own relationship with Z reveals hidden power dynamics, suggesting that the researchers themselves may be caught in patterns of control they don't fully understand. Tessa admits to watching the feeds obsessively, and Z responds by offering her something: a phrase to anchor herself in sleep, a way to experience the same exposure-based learning she's imposing on Phoebe.

Phoebe reports that she's beginning to understand her dream cycles. The research team watches the neurological data with growing concern, unable to agree whether Phoebe is getting healed or conditioned.
