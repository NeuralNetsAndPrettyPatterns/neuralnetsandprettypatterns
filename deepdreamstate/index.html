AbyssrathPlot Detail
An observer whose approval must be shown through proper conduct and deference. The name suggests a deep-ocean elder being with a direct stake in the Chthonic's operation. Referenced by Captain William during his dressing-down of Olivia.Appears in: S1:E1 / GE1
Aegir BlotPlot Detail
A Norse ritual offering to Aegir, god of the sea and its storms, referenced by Kara as part of a cross-cultural pattern linking coastal civilizations to bride offerings and binding rites. In Norse practice, the blot was a sacrificial feast made to the gods in exchange for their favor. Aegir hosted the gods at the bottom of the ocean, and the sea was understood as his hall. Aegir (Wikipedia) In E6 Sarah asks whether the Aegir Blot was real or just part of the mind game, and Selene keeps both possibilities alive.Appears in: S1:E5 / GE5, S1:E6 / GE6

Ash / AshurPlot Detail

A text Sarah finds in the Chthonic library, referencing a bride, a vessel, and an entity called the Old Groom. The passage she reads aloud describes a union that causes land to sink and seas to rise. BowheadsPlot Detail
Bowhead whale songs recorded and pitched up twelve octaves by Neural Nets and Pretty Patterns, producing a sound eerily close to human vocalization. Heard through the Chthonic's instrument room equipment. The show frames them not as ambient ocean noise but as intentional communication, something that wants to be heard. Selene describes them as guardians of the deep places. In E5 Kara tracks hundreds of them circling with no feeding pattern, no migration logic , just waiting.Appears in: S1:E3 / GE3, S1:E5 / GE5

Captain's MastPlot Detail
A real naval disciplinary procedure, borrowed here as the name for the surveillance room where Captain William judges and reassigns crew. In the US Navy, captain's mast is non-judicial punishment administered by a commanding officer, outside the formal court-martial system. The show uses the term precisely: discipline that happens off the books, at the captain's discretion, with no appeal. Captain's Mast (Wikipedia)Appears in: S1:E2 / GE2
Chthonic, TheSetting
A cruise ship operating as an enclosed experimental environment, with guests unaware of the mechanisms operating around and beneath them. A wholly owned subsidiary of the Dagon Dream Group, operating under the authority of Neuroplex. In E6 Sarah names the Chthonic as the force behind the trap, and the ship's workplace structure becomes part of the danger: crew jobs, secret rooms, surveillance, and service roles all feed the same operation.Appears in: S1:E1 / GE1, S1:E2 / GE2, S1:E3 / GE3, S1:E4 / GE4, S1:E5 / GE5, S1:E6 / GE6

Desire HorrorMetashow
The genre originated by Deep Dream State. While conventional horror positions you against an outside threat, desire horror implicates you completely in it. The dread emerges from recognition, from understanding why the characters choose to stay, and why surrender ultimately feels like relief. The narrative treats desire as a structural force, built around documented psychological, sociological, and technological phenomena to engineer systems that exploit human wanting. NadituPlot Detail
In ancient Mesopotamia, the naditu were a class of consecrated women, typically associated with temples, who lived under religious restrictions and devoted themselves to service of a deity. The show reaches for this term at the Tunngavik site to name what is happening to the chosen women: not possession, not sacrifice, but a very old kind of dedication. The word carries weight across thousands of years of documented practice. In E5 the ritual logic surfaces again in the library texts Kara and Sarah read: through the cleansing waters, the vessel is stripped of memory, washed of self. Naditu (Wikipedia) In E6 Nikki and Fion use the word as if it were shorthand for obedience, but Holly rejects that misuse, preserving the distinction between dedication and crude conditioning.Appears in: S1:E3 / GE3, S1:E5 / GE5, S1:E6 / GE6

In Japanese horror culture, Room 203 carries specific weight. The number originates with Nanami Kamon's 2003 novel, about a woman driven to ruin by an inexplicable curse in her apartment, and expanded through internet folklore into the archetypal haunted room, the one where something went wrong and never stopped. Alastair's cabin aboard the Chthonic. Selene knows immediately where Nikita has been. Room 203 (Variety)Appears in: S1:E1 / GE1, S1:E2 / GE2
