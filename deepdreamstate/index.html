The Show

Why do we like being controlled?

Deep Dream State is a full-cast serialized audio drama by Neural Nets and Pretty Patterns, built as desire horror across linked seasons of the Neuralverse.

It asks one core question.

"If it's horror, why do we like it so much?"

Horror and erotica have their own stock answers. Both come up short. Both genres fixate on one side of the coin, so they can't see where they fuse.

I'm not sure we can get an answer, but I know it's worth asking the question. Almost every contemporary control system runs on enjoyment. Horror used to be about what we ran from. Now, the genuinely unsettling stuff is what we run towards.

Deep Dream State poses this question again and again. Each of the first four seasons is a fresh take on the same question.

The Seasons
S1  ·  GE 1–9
Chthonic

Follows a luxury cruise wedding party as they sail into something older than ceremony. The descent begins with social discomfort and ends in absolute surrender to forces that predate language. This season asks why rituals feel simultaneously threatening and seductive, why we seek structures that dissolve individual will.

S2  ·  GE 10–15
Incognitoh

Drops contestants into a reality competition designed to remake its players from the inside out. The game uses surveillance not just to watch but to rewrite, turning visibility itself into the mechanism of control. Alliances become currency, manipulation becomes gameplay, and eventually the contestants can't distinguish between performing for cameras and simply being.

S3  ·  GE 16–27
Sitri Center

Offers participants entry into a dream research institute promising mastery over their unconscious minds. What begins as clinical treatment becomes orchestrated fantasy, and what seemed like liberation reveals itself as sophisticated imprisonment. Dreams that felt like escape routes become corridors of someone else's design.

S4  ·  Patreon
Solstitial

Explored gift-giving, obligation, and seasonal rituals we perform without understanding their origins or implications. The winter solstice marks a threshold where ancient practices resurface through modern celebration. What happens when the old ways remember themselves through us?

S5  ·  GE 28+  ·  Ongoing
Vale Four

The fourth season is commerce horror. An invisible institutional architecture has weaponized desire at scale, and the arc follows one calculated descent into absolute compliance. The system doesn't ask. It already knows. Ongoing.

About the Structure

Season, Episode, GE

Season and Arc mean the same thing in Deep Dream State. Episode numbers reflect where an episode falls within its season. GE is the Global Episode number, a running count across the entire series, the same number you see on IMDb and MusicBrainz. Cycles group episodes by theme within a season. They don't change any numbers. They're additional context for the storyline you're in. Season, Episode, and GE are all you need.
